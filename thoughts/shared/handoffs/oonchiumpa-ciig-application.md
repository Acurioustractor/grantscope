## Project Concept: "On Country, On Purpose"

### The Pitch (1 paragraph)

Oonchiumpa will establish a place-based Aboriginal enterprise and cultural retreat hub in Alice Springs / At Napa station, Central Australia. The project creates sustainable employment for Aboriginal young people through two production enterprises — bed assembly/refurbishment and washing machine reconditioning — while simultaneously operating on-country cultural retreat programs at the historic At Napa homestead for school groups, justice-system-involved youth, and community. The innovation is combining circular economy enterprise (goods production) with on-country healing and cultural connection in one integrated model — young people work in enterprise during the week and connect to country on weekends, creating a pipeline from justice diversion through to employment.

### Three Pillars

**1. Goods Production Enterprise** (employment + revenue)
- Bed assembly and distribution (100 beds from Senegal already received, ongoing supply)
- Washing machine reconditioning and distribution
- Operating from shipping container workshop (Alice Springs initially, potentially Tanya's block)
- Employment: 4-6 casual/part-time positions for Aboriginal young people
- Revenue model: sell refurbished goods to remote communities, housing providers, organisations
- Partnership with A Curious Tractor Foundation (supply chain, logistics, market access)

**2. Creative Enterprise** (employment + cultural expression)
- Screen printing workshop in shipping container
- Aboriginal design merchandise — shirts, prints, cultural products
- Employment: 2-3 casual positions, primarily young Aboriginal women
- Revenue: direct sales, markets, online, wholesale to tourism outlets

**3. At Napa On-Country Retreat** (cultural impact + programs)
- Historic homestead restoration as cultural museum and gathering place
- On-country camps for youth justice diversion, school groups, community
- Accommodation infrastructure (bathroom upgrade, deck, paths, camping facilities)
- Cultural programs: history walks, storytelling, bush skills, Elder-led activities
- Filming and documentation of young people's stories
- Revenue: fee-for-service school groups, government program contracts
