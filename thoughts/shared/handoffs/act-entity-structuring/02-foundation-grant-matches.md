## Approach Strategy

### Phase 1: Foundation Pipeline (Now – Sep 2026)

**Target: 3–5 foundation applications within 6 months of Pty Ltd registration**

1. **Build relationships first** — most large foundations are invitation-only
   - Attend philanthropy sector events (Philanthropy Australia conference)
   - Connect via existing networks (Nic's OAM network, Ben's tech network)
   - Use AKT charity status as credibility signal

2. **Lead with The Harvest story** — most fundable narrative
   - Community venue in regional QLD
   - Arts + food + nature + technology
   - First Nations partnership on Country
   - Community ownership transition at Year 5
   - Visual, tangible, photographable

3. **JusticeHub for justice-focused foundations**
   - Paul Ramsay Foundation (justice reinvestment)
   - Snow Foundation (social disadvantage)
   - Australian Communities Foundation (justice sub-funds)

4. **Farm R&D for environment/research foundations**
   - Burnett Mary (QLD environment)
   - Reef & Rainforest Research Centre
   - Landcare Smart Farms program

### Phase 2: Diversified Funding (Year 2+)

| Source | Target | Entity |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Foundation grants | $200K/yr | AKT (charity) |
| Government grants | $150K/yr | Pty Ltd + AKT |
| R&D Tax Incentive | $170K/yr | Pty Ltd |
| Social enterprise procurement | $100K/yr | Pty Ltd (Social Traders certified) |
| Corporate partnerships | $50K/yr | Either |
| **Total non-revenue funding** | **$670K/yr** | |

### Phase 3: DGR Status (Critical Unlock)

**After Pty Ltd is established, apply for DGR status for AKT.** This unlocks:
- Tax-deductible donations from individuals
- Corporate giving programs (most require DGR)
- Philanthropic foundation grants that require DGR
- Crowdfunding with tax deductibility
- Workplace giving programs

DGR categories to apply under:
- **Item 1 — Public Benevolent Institution** (broadest category)
- Or **Item 4 — Cultural Organisation** (for arts-focused giving)
