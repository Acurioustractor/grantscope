## Part 2: Family Trusts — Tax-Efficient Founder Payments

### Why Two Trusts?

| Benefit | How It Works |
|---------|-------------|
| **Income splitting** | Distribute dividends to family members in lower tax brackets |
| **Asset protection** | Trust assets separate from personal liability |
| **Flexibility** | Trustee decides distribution annually based on circumstances |
| **R&D double benefit** | Pty Ltd claims R&D offset on wages → pays dividends to trust → trust distributes at lower rates |

### Structure

```
ACT Ventures Pty Ltd
  │
  ├── 50% shares → Ben's Family Trust
  │     Trustee: Ben (or corporate trustee)
  │     Beneficiaries: Ben, wife, children, future children
  │     └── Annual distribution to beneficiaries at their marginal rates
  │
  └── 50% shares → Nic's Family Trust
        Trustee: Nic (or corporate trustee)
        Beneficiaries: Nic, family members
        └── Annual distribution to beneficiaries at their marginal rates
```

### How Money Flows

```
Revenue → ACT Ventures Pty Ltd
  ├── Wages to Ben ($150K+) → PAYG withheld, super paid
  ├── Wages to Nic ($100K+) → PAYG withheld, super paid
  ├── Wages to family members (legitimate work only) → Market rates, documented
  ├── Operating expenses
  ├── R&D costs → 43.5% refund claimed annually
  ├── 40% profit → A Kind Tractor LTD (charitable arm)
  ├── Dividends → Ben's Family Trust (50% of remaining)
  │     └── Trust distributes to Ben's family at their marginal rates
  └── Dividends → Nic's Family Trust (50% of remaining)
        └── Trust distributes to Nic's family at their marginal rates
```

### ATO Red Flags to Avoid

| Risk | What Not to Do | What to Do Instead |
|------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Sham employment** | Pay family members for no work | Document hours, roles, deliverables — genuine market-rate work |
| **Division 7A** | Lend company money to trust informally | Formal loan agreements, minimum interest rate, 7-year max term |
| **Under-18 distributions** | Distribute unearned income to children | Only pay children for genuine work (data entry, testing, event help) at appropriate rates |
| **Unreasonable salaries** | Pay directors $30K and take rest as dividends | Pay market-rate salary FIRST, then distribute profits |
| **Missing records** | No timesheets for family work | Weekly timesheets, task descriptions, hourly rates documented |

### Setup Steps (Each Trust)

- **Refundable tax offset** = company tax rate (25%) + 18.5% = **43.5%** of eligible R&D spend
- This is a **cash refund**, not just a deduction — even if the company makes a loss
- Must register with AusIndustry within 10 months of FY end

## Part 6: Alternative Structures Considered

### Option A: Single Pty Ltd + Trusts (RECOMMENDED — current plan)

```
Pty Ltd (operating) ← trusts as shareholders
AKT LTD (charity arm) ← receives 40% of Pty Ltd profit
```

**Pros:** Simple, R&D eligible, grant eligible via both entities, clear pathway to community ownership
**Cons:** AKT sitting dormant costs ~$55/yr in ASIC fees

### Option B: Cooperative Model

```
ACT Cooperative Ltd (registered under Cooperatives National Law)
```

**Pros:** Democratic governance, community ownership built-in, some grant advantages
**Cons:** Cannot claim R&D Tax Incentive (cooperatives are excluded), harder to attract investment, governance overhead, limited flexibility on distributions

**Verdict:** Ruled out. R&D Tax Incentive eligibility alone is worth ~$170K/yr — far exceeds any cooperative grant advantages.

### Option C: Social Enterprise Certification (B Corp or similar)

```
ACT Ventures Pty Ltd (B Corp certified or Social Traders certified)
```

**Pros:** Access to social procurement channels (government social enterprise panels), marketing/brand credibility, impact investor access
**Cons:** Certification process takes 3–6 months, annual fees ($500–5K depending on revenue)

**Verdict:** Worth pursuing AFTER Pty Ltd is established and operating. Social Traders certification is particularly valuable in Australia — gets you on government social procurement panels. B Corp is better for international credibility. Can do both.

### Option D: Indigenous-Specific Structures

Given the partnership with Jinibara people and the work with Oonchiumpa:

| Structure | When to Consider |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Joint venture with Aboriginal corporation** | If Jinibara community wants formal partnership on Farm activities |
| **Indigenous Land Use Agreement (ILUA)** | If Farm activities require formal cultural authority |
| **Supply Nation registration** | If any Aboriginal ownership or substantial Aboriginal employment |
| **Indigenous Procurement Policy (IPP)** | Requires 50% Aboriginal ownership — not applicable to ACT directly |

**Verdict:** These aren't structures for ACT itself, but partnership frameworks to formalise the Jinibara relationship on the Farm. Worth discussing with Traditional Owners when ready.

### Option E: Community Trust (Year 5 Goal)

```
ACT Community Trust (established ~2029-2030)
  └── Receives ownership of The Harvest + The Farm assets
  └── Governed by community board
  └── ACT founders step back from operational control
```

**Verdict:** This is the endgame. Build the Pty Ltd now to create value, then transfer assets to community trust at Year 5. The constitution of the Pty Ltd should include clauses enabling this transition.

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |

### 2. Lease Risk (Harvest)

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Landlord terminates early | Low (philanthropist motivation) | Lose $100K+ in improvements, lose venue | Negotiate clawback clause, minimum 5-year commitment |
| Revenue share dispute | Medium | Unexpected rent increases | Clearly define gross revenue exclusions in lease |
| Personal guarantee required | Medium | Founders personally liable | Negotiate cap (e.g., 6 months rent) or guarantee release after 2 years |

### 3. Family Trust Risk

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| ATO challenges trust distributions | Medium (common audit target) | Tax reassessment + penalties | Follow PCG 2021/4 guidance, document legitimate work |
| Division 7A breach | High if not properly managed | Deemed unfranked dividends + penalties | Never lend company money to trust without compliant loan agreement |
| Relationship breakdown between founders | Low but catastrophic | Deadlock on 50/50 structure | Shareholder agreement with buy-sell provisions, dispute resolution |

### 4. Dual Entity Complexity

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Revenue misallocation (Pty vs AKT) | Medium | Tax/charity compliance issues | Clear transfer pricing agreement, separate accounting |
| AKT compliance lapse | Medium (dormant entities get forgotten) | ACNC deregistration, lose charity status | Calendar reminders for ASIC review + ACNC statement |
| 40% profit transfer too generous | Medium in early years | Cash flow problems for Pty Ltd | Make 40% contingent on minimum retained earnings |

### 5. Community Ownership Transition

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Assets worth more than expected at transfer | Medium | CGT event, value extraction challenges | Plan early, use market-value buy-back over time |
| Community trust governance failure | Medium (common in community orgs) | Asset mismanagement post-transfer | Strong constitution, training, staged transition |
| Founders can't let go | Personal risk | Delayed transition, mission drift | Fixed timeline in shareholder agreement, external accountability |
