## Purpose

This pack is the working draft for how to approach the Snow Foundation with a focused, credible offer.

The offer is not "buy a new platform."

The offer is:

1. GrantScope / CivicGraph can make Snow's portfolio, capital posture, grant network, and place-based patterns legible.
2. Empathy Ledger can make the same portfolio intelligible through lived evidence, culturally safe story capture, and project-specific analysis.
3. Together, the stack can help Snow make better decisions, report more honestly, and show what is actually happening in the places and systems they care about.

## Core Thesis

Snow is a strong fit because their public strategy already points toward the exact combination this stack can provide:

- place-based funding
- gap-finding in the funding landscape
- community-led initiatives
- long-term partnership
- gender-informed decision-making
- social justice and systems change
- RHD, housing, and social determinants
- shared intelligence and outcomes thinking

This means the first conversation should not be "would you like software?"

It should be:

"We assembled a live portfolio intelligence and community evidence view of Snow's work across RHD, place-based funding, and community investment. We think it can help you see your own portfolio more clearly."

## The Offer To Snow

Do not position this as:

- a generic grants platform
- a storytelling app
- a dashboard build
- a new reporting burden

Position it as:

### "Portfolio intelligence plus community evidence"

GrantScope gives Snow:

- a live map of grants, programs, themes, geographies, and related entities
- a clearer view of where their public portfolio is concentrated
- a way to inspect openness, approachability, and system posture across philanthropy
- a way to connect foundation work to procurement, place, company, and entity intelligence where relevant

Empathy Ledger gives Snow:

- lived evidence from projects and communities
- culturally safer story and media collection
- project-specific outcome tracking
- a bridge from internal learning to external narrative

Together:

- Snow can see where money is going
- Snow can hear what those investments sound like in real communities
- Snow can report and decide from the same evidence base
