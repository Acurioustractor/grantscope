# Call Scripts — ORIC Registration

## Call 1: Aboriginal Investment NT (1800 943 039)

**Who should call:** Kristy or Tanya (Aboriginal voice carries more weight)
**When:** This week — before starting ORIC registration
**Purpose:** Confirm whether ORIC registration is actually required

### Script

> "Hi, my name is [Kristy Bloomfield / Tanya Turner]. I'm calling about the Community Impact & Innovation Grant Round 2.
>
> I run Oonchiumpa Consultancy & Services — we're an Aboriginal-led organisation in Alice Springs doing youth justice diversion and on-country cultural programs. We've been funded by NIAA for the last few years.
>
> I have a question about eligibility. Our current entity is a Pty Ltd — ABN 53 658 668 627. It's 100% Aboriginal-owned and controlled by Aboriginal directors. We want to apply for the CIIG for up to $1 million for our enterprise and on-country program.
>
> **Question 1:** Can an Aboriginal-controlled Pty Ltd apply, or does the applicant need to be a registered not-for-profit or ORIC corporation?
>
> **Question 2:** If we need to be an ORIC corporation, and we submit our ORIC registration before the 30 April deadline but it's still being processed, can we still lodge the CIIG application?
>
> **Question 3:** Would an auspice arrangement with an existing NFP be acceptable if our own registration isn't complete by the deadline?
>
> **Question 4:** Is the eligibility application a separate first step, or does it all go in together on 30 April?
>
> Thank you. Can I get your name so I can reference this conversation in our application?"

## Call 4 (FALLBACK): Auspice Partner

**Only explore if:** Both ORIC and NT Inc. Assoc. timelines are too tight.

Potential auspice partners in Alice Springs:
- **Tangentyere Council** — large Aboriginal org, broad mandate
- **Central Australian Aboriginal Congress** — health-focused but strong infrastructure
- **CASSE** (Central Australian Social & Sporting Enterprises) — social enterprise focus, natural fit
- **Children's Ground** — place-based, intergenerational, similar philosophy

> "Hi, I'm [Kristy/Tanya] from Oonchiumpa. We're applying for the Aboriginal Investment NT Innovation Grant — up to $1M for youth enterprise and on-country programs in Alice Springs.
>
> We need a not-for-profit entity to be the applicant. Would [your org] be willing to auspice our application? We'd run the project — you'd be the legal applicant and manage the financial administration.
>
> We're also registering our own ORIC corporation but the timeline is tight. This would be a temporary arrangement until our own registration comes through.
>
> Can we meet to discuss?"

## After the Calls — Decision Tree

```
Aboriginal Investment NT says Pty Ltd is OK?
  → YES: Apply as Pty Ltd. Still register ORIC for future grants.
  → NO: Continue below.

ORIC says registration will be done by 30 April?
  → YES: Register ORIC. Apply CIIG as new corporation.
  → MAYBE: Register ORIC AND arrange auspice as backup.
  → NO: Arrange auspice OR register NT Inc. Assoc. (faster).

Aboriginal Investment NT accepts auspice?
  → YES: Find auspice partner. Apply via them.
  → NO: NT Inc. Assoc. is the only path.
```
