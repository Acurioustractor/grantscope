## 1. Name

The name of the corporation is: **Oonchiumpa Aboriginal Corporation**.

---

## 2. Objectives

The objectives of the corporation are to assist in the relief of poverty, sickness, destitution, helplessness, distress, suffering, and misfortune among Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people, through the process of supporting social and economic development.

The corporation aims to:

**(a)** Provide culturally grounded youth empowerment and justice diversion programs for Aboriginal young people in Central Australia, including on-country cultural experiences, mentorship, education support, and pathways out of the justice system.

**(b)** Maintain and strengthen Aboriginal cultural knowledge, practices, and connection to country through Elder-led programs, on-country camps, cultural education, and intergenerational knowledge transfer, particularly at Atnarpa (Loves Creek/At Napa) Homestead and surrounding country.

**(c)** Create sustainable employment opportunities for Aboriginal people through community-based social enterprises, including goods production, refurbishment and redistribution, creative enterprises such as screen printing, and cultural tourism services.

**(d)** Provide culturally appropriate support services, cultural brokerage, and service navigation for Aboriginal people and families across Central Australia, supporting them to access services and navigate both cultural and contemporary worlds.

**(e)** Support young Aboriginal people experiencing housing insecurity through accommodation programs, family-centred support, and pathways to stable housing.

**(f)** Develop partnerships with universities, schools, government agencies, and other organisations to advance understanding of Aboriginal justice, culture, and self-determination.

**(g)** Develop and maintain community infrastructure including cultural facilities, enterprise workshops, on-country retreat facilities, and community gathering spaces on Aboriginal land.

**(h)** Operate and maintain a gift fund to be known as 'The Oonchiumpa Aboriginal Corporation Gift Fund' in accordance with the requirements of the *Income Tax Assessment Act 1997*.

## 3. Members

### 3.1 Who is eligible?

A member must be:

**(a)** at least 15 years old; and

**(b)** an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander person, **or** a person who supports the objectives of the corporation. However, a majority of members must be Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander people at all times.

Additional eligibility requirements:

**(c)** The person must support the objectives of the corporation.

**(d)** The person must usually live in the Central Australian region, or have a demonstrated connection to the corporation's work or community.

### 3.2 How to become a member

**(a)** A person applies in writing using the application form at Schedule 1 of this rule book.

**(b)** A person needs to be eligible under rule 3.1.

**(c)** The directors accept the application by resolution at a directors' meeting.

**(d)** The directors must consider all applications for membership within a reasonable period after they are received.

**(e)** The person's name, address and date they became a member is put on the register of members.

**(f)** The directors may refuse to accept a membership application. If they do so, they must write to the applicant about the decision and the reasons for it.

**(g)** A person does not become a member until their name is entered on the corporation's register of members. This must be done within 14 days after the directors accept the membership application. However, the corporation must not enter the person on the register of members until after the relevant general meeting or annual general meeting (AGM) has been held if:

- a person applies for membership after a notice has been given for a general meeting or AGM, and
- the general meeting or AGM has not been held when the directors consider the person's application.

### 3.3 Members' rights

A member can:

**(a)** attend, speak and vote at general meetings

**(b)** be made a director (if the member is eligible to be a director — see rule 5.3)

**(c)** put forward resolutions at general meetings, including under rule 4.6

**(d)** ask the directors to call a general meeting under rule 4.3

**(e)** look at the members' register free of charge

**(f)** look at the minutes of general meetings and AGMs free of charge

**(g)** look at the rule book or get a copy (free of charge)

**(h)** raise a dispute and have a dispute dealt with using rule 10

**(i)** look at the books of the corporation if the directors have authorised it or the members pass a resolution at a members' meeting which approves it.

### 3.4 Members' responsibilities

A member must:

**(a)** follow the corporation's rules

**(b)** let the corporation know if they change their address

**(c)** treat other members with respect.

Members should also attend general meetings (including AGMs) or give their apologies.

### 3.5 No membership fee

The members of the corporation are not required to pay fees to join or for ongoing membership of the corporation.

### 3.6 Liability of members

The members do not have to pay the corporation's debts if the corporation is wound up.

### 3.7 How to stop being a member

A person stops being a member if:

**(a)** they resign in writing

**(b)** they pass away

**(c)** their membership is cancelled in accordance with rule 3.8 or 3.9.

When a person stops being a member the corporation must put their name, address and the date they stopped being a member on the register of former members.

### 3.8 Cancelling membership

A person's membership can be cancelled by members passing a special resolution at a general meeting if the member:

**(a)** can't be contacted for two years

**(b)** misbehaves

**(c)** is not an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander person and the majority of members requirement under rule 3.1(b) is at risk of not being met.

The directors must give the person notice of the cancellation of their membership at the person's last known address as soon as possible after the special resolution is passed.

When a person's membership is cancelled the corporation must put their name, address and the date they stopped being a member on the register of former members.

### 3.9 Directors' limited right to cancel membership

For grounds not covered by rule 3.8, a person's membership can be cancelled by the directors passing a resolution at a directors' meeting if the member is not or stops being eligible for membership as set out in rule 3.1.

To do this, the directors must:

**(a)** write to the member to tell them:
- the directors are going to cancel their membership
- the member has 14 days to object to the planned cancellation
- if the member objects, they must write to the corporation to say so

**(b)** allow the member 14 days to object in writing to the intended cancellation.

If the member does not object, the directors must cancel the membership by passing a resolution at a directors' meeting. Then give the former member a copy of the resolution.

If the member objects, the directors cannot cancel the membership. The membership can only be cancelled by members passing a resolution at a general meeting.

### 3.10 The register(s) of members and former members

The register(s) must contain:

**(a)** the names and addresses of members and former members

**(b)** the date when each person's name was added to the register

**(c)** if a person is not an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander person

**(d)** for former members, the date when they stopped being a member.

The register(s) of members and former members must be kept at the corporation's document access address.

The register of members must be made available at the AGM.

## 4. General Meetings and AGMs (Members' Meetings)

### 4.1 AGM timing

An AGM must be held before the end of November each year.

### 4.2 AGM business

AGM business includes:

**(a)** checking the register of members

**(b)** confirming the minutes of the previous general meeting

**(c)** presenting reports: general, financial, directors'

**(d)** asking questions about how the corporation is managed

**(e)** electing directors (if required)

**(f)** choosing an auditor (if required) and agreeing on the fee.

### 4.3 Calling general meetings

The directors can call a general meeting or AGM by passing a resolution in a directors' meeting or by circulating resolution.

The required number of members can request the directors to call a general meeting:

| Number of members in corporation | Number of members required to request a general meeting |
|---|---|
| 2 to 10 members | 1 member |
| 11 to 20 members | 3 members |
| 21 to 50 members | 5 members |
| 51 members or more | 10 per cent of members |

The members' request must:

**(a)** be in writing

**(b)** state any resolutions to be proposed at the meeting

**(c)** be signed by the members making the request

**(d)** nominate a member to be the contact member on behalf of the members making the request

**(e)** be given to the corporation.

Within the 21 days of receiving the request the directors must either call the meeting or apply to the Registrar to deny the request.

#### Directors agree to the request

If the directors agree to the request they must call the general meeting within 21 days of receiving the members' request.

#### Directors apply to the Registrar to deny the request

If the directors resolve that:

- the request is frivolous or unreasonable, or
- complying with the request would be contrary to the interests of the members as a whole

a director, on behalf of all of the directors, may apply to the Registrar for permission to deny the request to call a general meeting.

The directors' application to the Registrar to deny the members' request must:

**(a)** be in writing

**(b)** set out the reasons why they wish to deny holding the meeting

**(c)** be made within 21 days after the members' request for a meeting was made.

The directors must give notice to the contact member that they have applied to the Registrar to deny the request.

### 4.4 General meeting business

General meetings business includes:

**(a)** confirming the minutes of the previous general meeting

**(b)** considering the business or resolutions in the notice of meeting.

### 4.5 Notice for general meetings and AGMs

At least **21 days' notice** must be given.

Notice must be given to:

**(a)** each member individually

**(b)** the directors

**(c)** the contact person or secretary

**(d)** the auditor (if the corporation has one).

The notice must set out:

**(a)** the place, date and time for the meeting

**(b)** the business of the meeting

**(c)** if a special resolution is being proposed, the exact wording of it

**(d)** any technology to be used in the meeting (if required)

**(e)** if a member can appoint a proxy.

Notices must be given to each member individually. This can be done by sending by post to their address, by fax, by email, by text message, or via social media. In addition to individual notice the corporation can also give notice in a manner which follows Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander custom, including notices on community noticeboards and via community radio.

A notice of meeting:

- sent by post is taken to be given three days after it is posted
- sent by fax, email, text, or other electronic means, is taken to be given on the business day after it is sent.

### 4.6 Members' resolutions

The required number of members can propose a resolution by giving notice of it to the corporation:

| Number of members in corporation | Number of members required to propose a resolution |
|---|---|
| 2 to 10 members | 1 member |
| 11 to 20 members | 3 members |
| 21 to 50 members | 5 members |
| 51 members or more | 10 per cent of members |

The notice must set out the resolution in writing and must be signed by the members proposing it.

The corporation must give notice of the resolution to all members in the same way as rule 4.5.

The corporation must consider the resolution at the next general meeting which is being held more than 28 days after the notice from the members has been given to the corporation.

### 4.7 Quorum at general meetings and AGMs

| Number of members in corporation | Number of members to make a quorum |
|---|---|
| 2 to 30 members | 2 members |
| 31 to 90 members | 5 members |
| 91 members or more | 10 members |

The quorum must be present during the whole meeting. If there is no quorum after one hour, the meeting is adjourned until the next week at the same time and at the same place. If there is still no quorum, the meeting is cancelled.

### 4.8 How to count the quorum

To work out if there is a quorum:

**(a)** count each member present at the meeting (if a member also holds a proxy, that member is only counted once)

**(b)** if rule 4.12 allows a non-member to hold a proxy for a member, count each non-member present at the meeting holding a proxy (if the non-member proxy holder holds more than one proxy, the non-member is only counted once)

**(c)** if a member has appointed more than one proxy and each of those proxy holders are at the meeting, count only one of them

**(d)** if a member has appointed one or more proxies and the member is also present at the meeting, do not count the member's proxy holders.

### 4.9 Chairing general meetings and AGMs

The directors can elect someone to chair the meeting. If they don't, the members must elect someone.

### 4.10 Using technology at general meetings and AGMs

General meetings and AGMs can be held at more than one place using any technology that gives members a way of taking part but the type of technology to be used must be set out in the notice of meeting.

### 4.11 Voting at general meetings and AGMs

**(a)** Each member has one vote.

**(b)** The chairperson has one vote (if he or she is a member) plus a casting vote.

**(c)** A challenge to a right to vote at a meeting may only be made at the meeting, and must be determined by the chairperson, whose decision is final.

**(d)** A resolution is decided by majority on a show of hands, unless a poll is demanded under rule 4.11(e). The chairperson tells the meeting whether they have received any proxy votes and how they are to be cast.

**(e)** Either the chairperson or any member entitled to vote on the resolution can demand a poll. A poll is a formal count of votes.

**(f)** A poll can be held instead of, or immediately after, a vote decided by majority on a show of hands.

**(g)** A poll demanded on any matter must be taken immediately. The chair of the meeting directs how the poll will be taken.

**(h)** The chairperson declares the results of the vote, on a show of hands, or when a poll is demanded.

### 4.12 Proxies at general meetings and AGMs

**(a)** Members can appoint another member as their proxy to attend meetings and vote for them.

**(b)** A proxy appointment must contain the member's name and address, the corporation's name, the proxy's name, the meeting where the proxy is going, and it must be signed by the member.

**(c)** An appointment of proxy form is at Schedule 2 of this rule book.

**(d)** The corporation must receive the proxy's appointment at least 48 hours before the meeting.

**(e)** A person must not be a proxy for more than three members.

### 4.13 Other people at general meetings and AGMs

The chairperson may allow any person other than a corporation director, member, proxy, or auditor to attend general meetings and AGMs. But the person cannot propose or vote on resolutions.

### 4.14 Postponing a general meeting or AGM

After notice has been given for a general meeting or AGM the directors can decide to postpone the meeting if there are exceptional reasons for doing so (such as sorry business, a natural disaster, or other cultural obligations).

The directors postpone the meeting by passing a resolution in a directors' meeting. A postponed meeting must be held within 30 days of the date that the meeting was due to occur.

The directors must give reasonable notice of the postponement and give each member individually a notice of the postponed meeting setting the new date, time and place.

## 5. Directors

### 5.1 Role of directors

The directors oversee the running of the corporation on behalf of all members, make decisions about the affairs of the corporation, and should always be aware of what the corporation and its employees are doing. The directors manage, or set the direction for managing, the business of the corporation.

The directors may exercise all the powers of the corporation except any that the CATSI Act or this rule book requires the corporation to exercise in a general meeting.

### 5.2 Number of directors

The number of directors of the corporation is between **3** and **7**, including up to **1** independent or specialist non-member director.

To change the number of directors, members need to pass a special resolution at a general meeting or AGM to change the rule book.

### 5.3 Eligibility of directors

A director (other than an independent director appointed under rule 5.7) must be:

**(a)** at least 18 years old

**(b)** a member of the corporation

**(c)** an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander person (however, up to 1 director may be a non-Aboriginal person if appointed as an independent or specialist director under rule 5.7. A majority of directors must be Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander people at all times.)

A person is not eligible to become a director if the person has been disqualified from managing corporations under the CATSI Act or the *Corporations Act 2001*.

### 5.4 Consent to act as director

A person must consent in writing to being appointed as a director before they can act as a director (see Schedule 3 — Consent to Become a Director Form).

### 5.5 Term of appointment

**(a)** Foundation directors hold office from registration until the first AGM.

**(b)** Directors elected at an AGM hold office for a term of **2 years** from the date of their election. A director is eligible for re-election.

**(c)** If a director's term of appointment expires before a replacement director is elected, the director continues to hold office until the close of the next AGM unless the director is removed from office under rule 5.10 or otherwise ceases to hold office.

### 5.6 Rotation of directors

At each AGM after the first AGM, one-third of the directors (rounded down) must retire by rotation. The directors who have held office longest since their last election must retire first. A retiring director is eligible for re-election.

### 5.7 Independent or specialist director

The directors may appoint up to **1** independent or specialist non-member director to bring particular skills or expertise to the board. This person:

**(a)** does not need to be a member but must consent in writing to the appointment

**(b)** does not need to be an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander person, provided the majority of directors remains Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander

**(c)** holds office until the next AGM, at which time the appointment must be confirmed or a new independent director appointed by the members.

### 5.8 How a person ceases to be a director

A person ceases to be a director if:

**(a)** they resign under rule 5.9

**(b)** they are removed under rule 5.10

**(c)** they are no longer a member of the corporation (unless they are an independent director under rule 5.7)

**(d)** they die

**(e)** they become disqualified from managing corporations under the CATSI Act

**(f)** they become of unsound mind

**(g)** they fail to attend three consecutive directors' meetings without leave of absence from the other directors.

### 5.9 Resignation of director

A director may resign from office by giving written notice of the resignation to the corporation. The resignation takes effect on the day the notice is received or at a later date stated in the notice.

### 5.10 Process for removing a director

#### Removal by members

Members can remove a director by passing an ordinary resolution at a general meeting. The members must give the director a reasonable opportunity to be heard at the meeting before the resolution is voted on.

#### Removal by other directors

The other directors can remove a director by passing a resolution at a directors' meeting if the director:

**(a)** has failed to comply with a material duty as a director

**(b)** has acted in a way that is significantly detrimental to the corporation.

The directors must give the director written notice that they propose to remove the director and give the director a reasonable opportunity to respond before the resolution is passed.

### 5.11 Directors' duties

Every director must:

**(a)** act honestly and in good faith in the best interests of the corporation

**(b)** act with reasonable care and diligence

**(c)** not use their position for personal advantage or the advantage of someone else

**(d)** not use information obtained through their position for personal advantage or the advantage of someone else

**(e)** disclose any conflicts of interest — including material personal interests in any matter the corporation is dealing with — to the other directors at a directors' meeting

**(f)** ensure the corporation does not trade while insolvent.

### 5.12 Directors' remuneration

Directors are not entitled to be paid for their services as directors unless the members pass a resolution at a general meeting authorising the payment. Directors are entitled to be reimbursed for reasonable expenses incurred in carrying out their duties as directors.

## 6. Contact Person or Secretary

### 6.1 Contact person or secretary

The corporation must have a **contact person** at all times. The corporation may also appoint a **secretary**.

### 6.2 Requirements

A person can be the contact person or secretary if they:

**(a)** are at least 18 years old

**(b)** live in Australia

**(c)** consent in writing to the appointment

**(d)** are not disqualified from managing a corporation under the CATSI Act.

A contact person or secretary does not need to be a member of the corporation but must be an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander person, or the directors must be satisfied the person will act in the interests of the corporation.

### 6.3 Duties

The contact person must pass on all communications — letters, emails, telephone calls and messages — to at least one director within 14 days of receiving them.

If the corporation has a secretary, the secretary must also:

**(a)** lodge with the Registrar: reports (general, financial and directors'), details of directors, and any changes to the corporation name, address, or rule book

**(b)** keep the register of members up to date

**(c)** take minutes at directors' meetings and general meetings.

## 7. Records

### 7.1 Minutes

The corporation must keep minutes of:

**(a)** all proceedings and resolutions of general meetings (including AGMs)

**(b)** all proceedings and resolutions of directors' meetings.

Minutes must be confirmed and signed by the chair of the meeting at which they were confirmed.

### 7.2 Financial records

The corporation must keep proper financial records that:

**(a)** correctly record and explain its financial transactions

**(b)** enable accurate financial statements to be prepared and audited (if required).

Financial records must be retained for at least **7 years** after the transactions they cover.

### 7.3 Register of members

The corporation must maintain a register of members and a register of former members as set out in rule 3.10.

### 7.4 Access to records

**(a)** Members can inspect the register of members and minutes of general meetings free of charge.

**(b)** Members can get a copy of the corporation's rule book free of charge.

**(c)** A copy of the rule book must be kept at the corporation's document access address.

## 8. Finances

### 8.1 Financial year

The financial year of the corporation ends on **30 June** each year.

### 8.2 Application of funds and property

The income and property of the corporation must be applied solely towards the promotion of its objectives as set out in rule 2. No portion of the income or property may be paid, transferred, or distributed directly or indirectly to any member of the corporation except as:

**(a)** reasonable remuneration for services actually rendered to the corporation

**(b)** reimbursement of expenses properly incurred on behalf of the corporation

**(c)** interest at a rate not exceeding market rate on money lent to the corporation

**(d)** reasonable rent for premises leased to the corporation by a member.

### 8.3 Bank accounts

**(a)** All money received by or on behalf of the corporation must be deposited as soon as practicable into a bank account held in the corporation's name.

**(b)** Payments from the corporation's accounts require authorisation by **two (2)** of the following: any director or the secretary.

### 8.4 Reporting

As a **small corporation** (revenue under $100,000, assets under $100,000, fewer than 5 employees) the corporation must:

**(a)** prepare a general report each year

**(b)** lodge the report with ORIC by the due date

**(c)** present financial reports at the AGM.

If the corporation's revenue or assets grow beyond the small corporation thresholds, the corporation must comply with the reporting requirements for medium or large corporations as applicable under the CATSI Act.

## 9. Application of Funds

### 9.1 Not-for-profit clause

The corporation is established as a not-for-profit body. The income and property of the corporation must be applied solely towards the promotion of its objectives and no portion shall be distributed to members except as permitted under rule 8.2.

### 9.2 No distribution to members

No member of the corporation shall receive any distribution, dividend, bonus or profit from the corporation's income or property. This does not prevent the payment of reasonable remuneration for services rendered or reimbursement of expenses as set out in rule 8.2.

## 10. Dispute Resolution

### 10.1 General

This rule applies to disputes between members and the corporation, or between members about anything to do with the corporation. This includes disputes about the management of the corporation and the operation of the rules.

### 10.2 Informal resolution and cultural process

**(a)** Before starting the formal process under this rule, the parties to a dispute should first try to resolve the dispute through direct, respectful discussion.

**(b)** Where culturally appropriate, the parties may seek the guidance of Elders or cultural authorities to assist in resolving the dispute through cultural mediation processes. The corporation recognises that cultural mediation following Aboriginal law and custom may be a more appropriate first step than formal processes.

### 10.3 Giving of dispute notice

If informal resolution is not successful, the party raising the dispute must give written notice of the dispute to the corporation. The notice must set out:

**(a)** what the dispute is about

**(b)** what outcome the party wants.

### 10.4 Seeking assistance from the Registrar

If the dispute cannot be resolved after following the steps in rules 10.2 and 10.3, any party may ask the Registrar for help to resolve the dispute.

### 10.5 Referring dispute to the directors

If the dispute is between members, the parties may refer the dispute to the directors. The directors must consider the dispute and make a decision, and must give written notice of their decision to all parties within 28 days.

### 10.6 Referring dispute to a general meeting

If the dispute is not resolved under rules 10.4 or 10.5, any party may refer the dispute to a general meeting for resolution by the members.

## 11. Changing the Rule Book

### 11.1 Special resolution required

These rules may be changed only by **special resolution** passed at a general meeting. A special resolution requires at least **75% of the votes cast** by members entitled to vote on the resolution.

### 11.2 Notice of proposed change

The notice of the general meeting at which the special resolution is to be proposed must set out the exact wording of the proposed change.

### 11.3 Lodging with the Registrar

A copy of the changed rules must be lodged with the Registrar within **28 days** of the special resolution being passed. The changes do not take effect until they are registered by the Registrar.

## 12. Gift Fund Rules

### 12.1 Name of the gift fund

The corporation must maintain a gift fund to be known as **'The Oonchiumpa Aboriginal Corporation Gift Fund'** (the gift fund).

### 12.2 Purpose of the gift fund

**(a)** The gift fund is established and maintained for the principal purpose of providing money, property, or benefits to the corporation for its purposes as set out in rule 2.

**(b)** The gift fund must be used only for the principal purpose of the corporation.

### 12.3 Requirements

**(a)** The gift fund must have its own bank account.

**(b)** Receipts must be issued in the name of the gift fund for all gifts to the fund.

**(c)** The gift fund must not receive any money or property other than:
- gifts of money or property to the fund
- money received because of those gifts
- interest or investment income from the fund.

**(d)** The corporation must keep proper records and accounts of the gift fund.

### 12.4 Winding up the gift fund

If the gift fund is wound up, any surplus money or assets remaining in the fund after payment of liabilities must be transferred to another fund, authority, or institution:

**(a)** that has similar objectives to the corporation

**(b)** to which income tax deductible gifts can be made.

---

## 13. Winding Up

### 13.1 Resolution to distribute surplus assets

If the corporation is wound up, any assets remaining after payment of all debts and liabilities must be transferred to another Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander corporation, or another not-for-profit body:

**(a)** that has similar objectives to this corporation

**(b)** whose rule book or constitution prevents it from distributing its income and property to its members.

The body to receive the surplus assets must be determined by the members at a general meeting or, if the members do not make a determination, by a court.

### 13.2 No distribution of surplus assets to members

No surplus assets may be paid, transferred, or distributed to any member of the corporation on winding up.

---

## 14. Cultural Authority

### 14.1 Recognition of cultural authority

The corporation acknowledges and respects the cultural authority of Traditional Owners and Elders of the Central Australian region. The corporation operates on the lands and waters of the Arrernte and other Aboriginal peoples of Central Australia.

### 14.2 Elder consultation

Decisions affecting cultural matters, country, cultural knowledge, and the cultural components of the corporation's programs must include meaningful consultation with relevant Elders and cultural authorities. The directors must ensure that:

**(a)** Elder cultural advisors are invited to provide guidance on all matters relating to culture, country, and traditional knowledge

**(b)** young people's programs incorporate Elder-designed cultural content

**(c)** cultural protocols are respected in all of the corporation's activities.

### 14.3 Cultural safety

The corporation is committed to maintaining cultural safety in all its programs and operations. This includes:

**(a)** ensuring programs are delivered in culturally appropriate ways

**(b)** supporting the use of Aboriginal languages in the corporation's programs

**(c)** recognising and respecting sorry business and cultural obligations.
