### 1.3 Objectives

The objectives of Oonchiumpa Aboriginal Corporation are:

1. **Youth empowerment and justice diversion** — To support Aboriginal young people in Central Australia through culturally grounded programs that provide alternatives to incarceration, including on-country cultural experiences, mentorship, and employment pathways.

2. **Cultural preservation and healing** — To maintain and strengthen Aboriginal cultural knowledge, practices and connection to country through Elder-led programs, on-country camps, cultural education, and intergenerational knowledge transfer, particularly at Atnarpa (Loves Creek) Homestead and surrounding country.

3. **Social enterprise and employment** — To create sustainable employment opportunities for Aboriginal people through community-based enterprises including goods production and refurbishment, creative enterprises, and cultural tourism services.

4. **Community wellbeing and service navigation** — To provide culturally appropriate support services, cultural brokerage, and service navigation for Aboriginal people and families across Central Australia, supporting them to access services and navigate both cultural and contemporary worlds.

5. **Housing and accommodation support** — To support young Aboriginal people experiencing housing insecurity through accommodation programs, family-centred support, and pathways to stable housing.

6. **Cultural education and partnerships** — To develop partnerships with universities, schools, government agencies, and other organisations to advance understanding of Aboriginal justice, culture, and self-determination.

7. **Community infrastructure** — To develop and maintain community infrastructure including cultural facilities, enterprise workshops, on-country retreat facilities, and community gathering spaces on Aboriginal land.

### 1.4 Not-for-Profit Clause
The corporation is established as a not-for-profit body. The income and property of the corporation must be applied solely towards the promotion of its objectives. No portion of the income or property may be paid, transferred, or distributed directly or indirectly to any member of the corporation except as:
- reasonable remuneration for services actually rendered to the corporation
- reimbursement of expenses incurred on behalf of the corporation
- interest at a rate not exceeding market rate on money lent to the corporation

### 1.5 Winding Up Clause
If the corporation is wound up, any assets remaining after payment of debts and liabilities must be transferred to another Aboriginal corporation or charitable body with similar objectives, as determined by the members at a general meeting.

## Part 2 — Membership

### 2.1 Eligibility
A person is eligible for membership if they:
- Are at least 15 years old
- Support the objectives of the corporation
- Are accepted by the directors

**Note:** The majority of members must be Aboriginal and/or Torres Strait Islander at all times, as required by the CATSI Act.

### 2.2 Application for Membership
A person seeking membership must:
1. Complete a membership application form
2. Be proposed by an existing member (or, for foundation members, self-nominate)
3. Have their application considered by the directors at their next meeting
4. Be notified in writing of the directors' decision

### 2.3 Register of Members
The corporation must maintain a register of members containing:
- Full name
- Date of birth
- Residential address
- Date membership began
- Whether Aboriginal and/or Torres Strait Islander

### 2.4 Cessation of Membership
A person ceases to be a member if they:
- Resign by written notice to the corporation
- Die
- Are removed by special resolution of the members (after being given an opportunity to be heard)

## Part 3 — Directors

### 3.1 Number of Directors
The corporation must have a minimum of **3 directors** and a maximum of **7 directors**.

### 3.2 Eligibility
A person is eligible to be a director if they:
- Are at least 18 years old
- Are a member of the corporation
- Are not disqualified under the CATSI Act (e.g. undischarged bankrupt, convicted of dishonesty offence)

**Majority requirement:** At least two-thirds (2/3) of directors must be Aboriginal and/or Torres Strait Islander at all times.

### 3.3 Election and Appointment
- Directors are elected at the Annual General Meeting (AGM) by a vote of members
- Foundation directors (first directors) are appointed at registration and serve until the first AGM
- Casual vacancies may be filled by the remaining directors until the next AGM

### 3.4 Term of Office
Directors hold office for a term of **2 years** from the date of their election and are eligible for re-election.

### 3.5 Office Bearers
The directors must elect from among themselves:
- **Chairperson** — presides at meetings, spokesperson for the corporation
- **Deputy Chairperson** — acts in the Chairperson's absence
- **Secretary** — maintains records, lodges documents with ORIC
- **Treasurer** — oversees financial management and reporting

One person may hold more than one office (except Chairperson and Deputy Chairperson).

### 3.6 Directors' Meetings
- Directors must meet at least **4 times per year** (quarterly)
- Quorum: majority of directors (e.g. 2 of 3, or 3 of 5)
- Meetings may be held in person, by phone, or by video conference
- Decisions are by majority vote; the Chairperson has a casting vote in the event of a tie
- Minutes must be kept of all directors' meetings

### 3.7 Duties of Directors
Directors must:
- Act honestly and in good faith
- Act in the best interests of the corporation
- Not use their position for personal advantage
- Disclose any conflicts of interest
- Not trade while insolvent

### 3.8 Removal of Directors
A director may be removed by:
- Ordinary resolution of members at a general meeting (after being given an opportunity to be heard)
- Resignation in writing to the corporation

## Part 4 — General Meetings

### 4.1 Annual General Meeting (AGM)
- The corporation must hold an AGM within **5 months** after the end of the financial year
- **First AGM** must be held within **3 months** of registration
- Business at AGM: receive financial reports, elect directors, appoint auditor/reviewer (if required), any other business

### 4.2 General Meetings
- May be called by the directors at any time
- Must be called if requested by at least **10% of members** (or 5 members, whichever is greater)
- **14 days written notice** required for all general meetings

### 4.3 Quorum
A quorum for general meetings is **5 members** present (in person or by proxy) or **50% of members**, whichever is less.

### 4.4 Voting
- Each member has one vote
- Decisions by ordinary resolution (simple majority) unless special resolution required
- Special resolutions require 75% of votes cast
- Voting may be by show of hands, voice, or secret ballot (if requested)

## Part 5 — Finance

### 5.1 Financial Year
The financial year of the corporation ends on **30 June** each year.

### 5.2 Bank Accounts
- All money received must be deposited into a bank account in the corporation's name
- Payments require the signatures/authorisation of **2 directors** (or 1 director and 1 authorised person)

### 5.3 Financial Records
The corporation must keep proper financial records that:
- Correctly record and explain its financial transactions
- Enable accurate financial statements to be prepared
- Are retained for at least 7 years

### 5.4 Reporting
As a **small corporation**, Oonchiumpa must:
- Prepare a general report each year (not a full financial audit)
- Lodge the report with ORIC by the due date
- Present financial statements at the AGM

## Part 6 — Other Matters

### 6.1 Common Seal
[Decide: Have a common seal? Recommendation: No — not required for small corporations and adds cost]

### 6.2 Disputes
Internal disputes should be resolved through:
1. Direct discussion between the parties
2. Mediation by a neutral member or Elder
3. If unresolved, referral to ORIC's dispute resolution service

### 6.3 Amendment of Rules
These rules may be amended by **special resolution** (75% of votes cast) at a general meeting. Any changes must be lodged with ORIC within **28 days**.

### 6.4 Cultural Authority
The corporation acknowledges and respects the cultural authority of Traditional Owners and Elders. Decisions affecting cultural matters, country, and cultural knowledge must include consultation with relevant Elders and cultural authorities.
