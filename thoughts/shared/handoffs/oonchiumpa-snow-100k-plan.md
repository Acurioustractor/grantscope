**"Test, Learn, Scale"**

**Entity:** Oonchiumpa Consultancy & Services (ABN 53 658 668 627)

**Leaders:** Kristy Bloomfield, Tanya Turner
**Period:** April – September 2026 (6 months)
**Funding type:** Untied capacity-building

---

## Overview

The Snow Foundation's $100,000 will fund a six-month sprint to test whether At Napa station can work as a commercial on-country cultural experience venue. Three events for three different audiences — judges, international investors, and community — will prove the model, build the brand, and generate the evidence needed to scale.

Oonchiumpa has two confirmed anchor events landing in April: a 50-person National Judicial College on-country program (April 17) and a visit from North American agricultural investors through Hewitt Agrimedia (April 21). These are real audiences paying real money. What's missing is the capacity to deliver them properly, capture the story, and turn one-off events into a repeatable commercial offering.

This funding fills that gap. A dedicated project manager will handle logistics, systems and communications so Kristy and Tanya can focus on cultural delivery and leadership. Travel funding gets them in front of universities, partners and communities who will become future clients. Event facilities and equipment hire means professional delivery without capital purchases. And video and marketing collateral turns every event into promotional material for the next one.

By September, Oonchiumpa will have delivered three tested events, established annual partnerships with the Hewitts and the National Judicial College, launched a NAIDOC on-country event, and built a commercial package ready to sell. That's the story for the next ask.

Snow's new financial year starts around September. The goal is to spend this $100K in six months, demonstrate clear momentum, and come back with a specific, evidence-based request for the next phase — likely $200K to scale what worked.

### Budget Detail

**Project Manager — $42,000**
Six-month contract for an Indigenous project manager. Their job: event logistics, systems and process setup, communications, community engagement. Contract-based with a clear brief — if this works, more work follows. Mentored by ACT team. Kristy and Kylie guide the work. This is the single most important line item: it buys back Kristy and Tanya's time to focus on cultural leadership and strategic relationships instead of admin.

**Leadership travel & engagement — $10,000**
Kristy and Tanya visiting universities, meeting potential corporate and institutional partners, engaging communities, attending events that generate leads. Every trip builds relationships that bring people back to At Napa. Flights, accommodation, meals.

**Event facilities & equipment hire — $14,000**
Everything needed to deliver three professional events without buying assets: marquee and shade hire, AV and sound equipment, portable amenities, vehicle hire for transport to station, tables and chairs, generator hire. Rental, not purchase — keeps it operational.

**Video & marketing collateral — $14,000**
Filmmaker for judges day and Americans week. Professional footage, participant testimonials (especially international visitors on camera — killer promo material). Cut into: promotional video, website content, social media assets, printed materials. By July, Oonchiumpa has a commercial package it can send to anyone who asks "what do you offer?"

**Events (catering, entertainment, logistics) — $8,000**
Bush tucker and catering for three events (judges, Americans, NAIDOC). Entertainment and dancers for NAIDOC. Transport logistics, materials, supplies. Stage build for NAIDOC (wood, simple, cheap).

**Systems, comms, CRM — $3,000**
Booking system, email setup, communications plan, basic CRM so enquiries don't get lost. PM works with ACT team on this. The operational backbone that makes everything repeatable.

**Evaluation & review — $3,000**
Short impact story — not a compliance report, but a narrative document with photos, quotes, numbers, and learnings. The thing you hand to Snow in September that tells the six-month story and sets up the next ask.

**Contingency/reserve — $6,000**
Buffer for overruns or unexpected opportunities.

## Timeline

### Phase 1: April–May — Build & Test

- **April 17 — Judges Day**: 50 judges from the National Judicial College. Full day on country: Anzac Hill, Stuart Town Gaol, Emily Gap, Altunga, At Napa. Smoking ceremony, history talks, bush foods walk, bush medicine making, panel discussion, dinner, documentary screening. $1,000/head = $50,000 revenue.
- **April 21 — North American Investors**: Hewitt Agrimedia's international agricultural investors. Half-day (push for longer — overnight under stars would be the ultimate test). Cultural experience, homestead history, bush foods. Get them on camera talking about the experience.
- PM starts: sets up systems, manages logistics for both events, documents everything.
- Kristy and Tanya travel to engage university and institutional partners.

**Deliverables by end of May:**
- Two events delivered to two very different audiences
- Professional video footage and international testimonials
- Feedback forms from all participants
- PM has event playbook and systems documented

### Phase 2: June–July — NAIDOC & Commercial Package

- **NAIDOC Week (July 5–12)** — On-country event at At Napa. Two possible formats: community celebration day + separate corporate/Hewitt day. Test a public event alongside an invite-only one.
- Build commercial experience package from Phase 1 learnings (itinerary options, pricing tiers, promo video, website).
- Possible **official opening** of homestead history display — invite Snow, supporters, community leaders. Something they can point at and say "this is real."
- Establish annual Hewitt cultural partnership (NAIDOC visit becomes a fixture).

**Deliverables by end of July:**
- Three events across three audiences (legal, international, community/corporate)
- Commercial package ready to sell
- Annual partnerships established
- NAIDOC event becomes repeatable

### Phase 3: August–September — Review & Next Ask

- PM wraps up and hands over documentation.
- Impact story produced: what worked, what we learned, revenue generated, partnerships created.
- Scope up the next phase with specific costings based on evidence.

**September — Go back to Snow with:**
1. Six-month story: *"Your $100K funded three events, a project manager, and a commercial package. We now have annual partnerships with Hewitt and the National Judicial College, a NAIDOC event, and a tested model."*
2. Video montage of all three events
3. Revenue numbers (judges $50K + Americans + NAIDOC + any bookings generated)
4. Clear ask: *"We need $200K for [specific next phase] based on what we learned."*

## The Bigger Picture

This $100K is not about building a station. It's about proving that Oonchiumpa can deliver professional, culturally authentic on-country experiences to paying audiences — and that there's a market for it. If the answer is yes, the next ask to Snow (and others) becomes specific and evidence-based. If the answer is no, $100K of learning is far cheaper than $1M of guessing.

Snow gets exactly what they want: an ambitious organisation using untied funding to test something real, with regular updates and a clear pathway to sustainability.
