## What CivicGraph does

**CivicGraph** is the data infrastructure behind **JusticeHub** (justicehub.com.au). It links 559,000 entities across 7 government systems:

- $222 billion in federal procurement contracts
- $4.2 billion in state justice funding
- 312,000 political donation records
- 66,000 registered charities
- 10,800 foundations
- ATO tax transparency data
- The Australian Living Map of Alternatives (ALMA): 1,155 evidence-based interventions

Every entity is cross-referenced by ABN, name, and board membership. When the same organisation appears in procurement, justice funding, charity data, and ALMA evidence — we see it as one entity across systems. No single government department can do this.

**What this reveals for Indigenous communities:**

- **22x overrepresentation** of Indigenous children in NSW youth detention. 17x nationally. 65% haven't been sentenced.
- **$1.17 million/year** to detain one child in NSW. Community prevention programs with evidence cost a fraction.
- **1,129 community-controlled organisations** in the NT alone — but the money flows overwhelmingly to large non-Indigenous service providers.
- **Major cities receive 118x the dollar flow** of Very Remote areas ($2.3B vs $20M average per LGA).
- **22 ALMA interventions** mapped specifically for Alice Springs and Central Australia — Indigenous-led, evidence-backed, chronically underfunded.

This isn't a report. It's live infrastructure. Updated daily. Covering every state, every LGA, every dollar we can trace.

## How it powers real programs

CivicGraph is the intelligence layer. Here's what it enables:

**Contained** (justicehub.com.au/contained) — a touring shipping container that puts people inside the data. Three rooms: what detention looks like, what works instead, and who's already doing it. Every stop is customised with local CivicGraph data. Alice Springs shows Alice Springs numbers. The evidence is specific, local, and undeniable.

**Community exchanges** — already running. Judges are visiting Alice Springs for cultural tours and workshops with Oonchiumpa Aboriginal Corporation. Youth exchanges are next: young people from Alice Springs visiting Curious Tractor (our farm in SE Brisbane) for respite, workshops, and connections to programs in other communities. Then others visiting Alice Springs to learn from Indigenous-led models.

CivicGraph identifies which communities to connect. ALMA shows what evidence exists. The exchange happens. The case study feeds back into the evidence base. Communities learn to run their own models. They own them. They get paid properly.

## The path to self-sustaining

The model doesn't need philanthropic funding forever:

1. **Community organisations learn to use CivicGraph** to see their own data, benchmark against peers, and make evidence-based cases for funding.
2. **Government commissions reports** — the data they can't build themselves. Productivity Commission, AIHW, and state departments already use fragments of what we've consolidated.
3. **The exchange network grows** — communities fund their own exchanges through justice reinvestment savings. Less detention spend = more community spend. The economics work when you can prove it.

Your funding builds the infrastructure. The infrastructure makes the invisible visible. Visibility changes allocation. Changed allocation funds the next phase.
