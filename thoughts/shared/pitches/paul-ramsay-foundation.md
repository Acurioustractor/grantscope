## The data: what nobody else can see

**CivicGraph** is the data engine behind **JusticeHub** (justicehub.com.au). It links 559,000 entities across every major government spending system:

| System | Records | What it shows |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| Federal procurement | 770,000 contracts, $222B | Who government pays |
| State justice funding | 71,000 grants, $4.2B | Who delivers youth justice services |
| Political donations | 312,000 records | Who funds the politicians |
| Charities (ACNC) | 66,000 | Who's registered as doing good |
| Foundations | 10,800 | Who funds the charities |
| ATO transparency | 24,000 | Who pays tax (and who doesn't) |
| ALMA evidence | 1,155 interventions | What actually works |

Every entity cross-referenced by ABN and board membership. When the same organisation takes government contracts, receives justice funding, donates to political parties, and appears in the charity register — we see it as **one entity across all systems**.

No single government department can do this. That's by design. CivicGraph breaks the silos.

## What the linked data reveals

**The money flows to the wrong places:**
- Community-controlled Aboriginal organisations receive **3.2% of procurement dollars** despite delivering better outcomes in every evaluation we can find
- Major cities receive **118x the dollar flow** of Very Remote areas
- The top 20 youth justice service providers in NSW are large non-Indigenous organisations. The community organisations with evidence are at the bottom of the funding table.

**The alternatives work and cost almost nothing by comparison:**
- Detention: **$3,200/day** ($1.17M/year) — 40% recidivism
- Community supervision: **$368/day** ($134K/year)
- Community prevention programs with evidence (ALMA): **as low as $75/day** — recidivism rates under 15% in programs using therapeutic and cultural connection models

**1,155 evidence-based interventions** are mapped through ALMA with methodology, outcomes, and links to the organisations delivering them. 22 specifically for Alice Springs and Central Australia. These aren't theoretical. They're running, they have evidence, and they're underfunded.

## The program: justice reinvestment made real

This isn't a policy paper. It's operational.

**Contained** (justicehub.com.au/contained) — A shipping container touring Australian cities. Three rooms, 30 minutes. Room 1: what detention is. Room 2: what works (ALMA evidence, local data). Room 3: the community orgs already doing it, and how little they receive. Every stop customised with CivicGraph data from that community.

**Community exchanges** — Judges are already visiting Alice Springs for cultural tours and workshops with Oonchiumpa Aboriginal Corporation. They see kinship systems, community-led programs, and Indigenous youth work that no report can convey.

Next: **youth exchanges.** Young people from Alice Springs visiting Curious Tractor — our farm in South East Brisbane — for respite, energy, workshops, and connection. Then communities visiting Alice Springs to learn from Indigenous-led models. CivicGraph identifies which communities to connect. ALMA shows what evidence exists. The exchange creates the case study. The case study feeds back into the evidence base.

Two hubs now. Curious Tractor (Brisbane) and Alice Springs (Oonchiumpa). More as the network grows.
