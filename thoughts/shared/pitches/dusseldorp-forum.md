## What's live right now

**JusticeHub** (justicehub.com.au) is the public face of a justice data infrastructure that doesn't exist anywhere else in Australia. Behind it sits **CivicGraph** — a platform linking 559,000 entities across 7 government systems (procurement, justice funding, political donations, charity, foundations, tax transparency, and the Australian Living Map of Alternatives).

Some of what it shows:

- **$1.17 million per year** to detain one child in NSW. $3,200/day. 200 children. 72% haven't been sentenced.
- **22x Indigenous overrepresentation** in NSW youth detention. Nationally, 17x.
- **1,155 evidence-based alternatives** mapped through ALMA — linked to the organisations delivering them and the funding flowing to them.
- **Community-controlled organisations receive 3.2% of government procurement dollars** despite delivering better outcomes in every evaluation we can find.

This isn't a research project. It's live infrastructure, updated daily, covering every state.

**Contained** takes this data and puts people inside it — literally. A shipping container touring Australian cities. Three rooms, 30 minutes:

1. **Current Reality** — designed by local young people. What detention looks and feels like.
2. **What Works** — ALMA evidence. Therapeutic models that achieve 13.6% recidivism vs 40%+ nationally.
3. **Who's Already Doing It** — CivicGraph data showing the community orgs, the funding they receive (or don't), and what their evidence says.

Every stop is customised with local data from CivicGraph. Mount Druitt shows Mount Druitt numbers. Alice Springs shows Alice Springs numbers. The data is real, local, and undeniable.

## The community exchange — already running

We're not pitching a concept. Judges are already travelling to Alice Springs for cultural tours and workshops run through our network with Oonchiumpa Aboriginal Corporation. They see kinship systems, community-led programs, and Indigenous approaches to youth support that no policy paper can convey.

The next step: **youth exchanges.** Young people from Alice Springs visiting Curious Tractor — our farm in South East Brisbane — for respite, energy, workshops, and connection to programs in other communities. Then communities visiting Alice Springs to learn from Indigenous-led models and take insights back to their own context.

CivicGraph identifies which communities to connect and why. ALMA shows what evidence exists at each end. The exchange creates the case study. The case study feeds back into the evidence base. The evidence base informs the next exchange.

Two hubs now — Curious Tractor (Brisbane) and Alice Springs (Oonchiumpa). More as the network grows.
