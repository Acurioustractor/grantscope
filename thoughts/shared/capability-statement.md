## What we do

CivicGraph is Australia&rsquo;s accountability atlas — the only civic data
infrastructure that resolves Australian entities across AusTender, the
AEC donation registers, ACNC, GrantConnect, ABR, and the ATO tax
transparency datasets into a single cross-referenced graph.

We build this as a public good. Community organisations, journalists, and
researchers use it free. Institutions commission custom research, briefings,
and bespoke analysis — that revenue funds the infrastructure for everyone
else.

---

## Data scale (current)

- **559,000** resolved entities
- **1,500,000** cross-system relationships (contracts, donations, grants,
  directorships, lobbying, shared-director links)
- **770,000** government contracts indexed
- **312,000** political donation records
- **71,000** justice-system funding records
- **18,500,000** ABN records (full bulk ABR extract)
- **237,000** unique people mapped to organisations via board roles
- **1,155** evidence-based interventions catalogued (ALMA)
- **492** local government areas with per-LGA funding aggregates
- **12,000** postcodes with SEIFA + remoteness + funding overlays

Coverage includes community-controlled and Indigenous-led flags, IPP
procurement context, and place-based (LGA/postcode) aggregation for any
query.

## What makes it unique

- Entity resolution across **every** public Australian dataset. Nobody
  else does this.
- Cross-system flags (community-controlled, Indigenous-led, donor-contractor,
  revolving-door) that make patterns visible at a glance.
- Place-based aggregation down to postcode and LGA.
- Two years of pipeline infrastructure; 289 data agents running monthly.
- Investigative methodology tested against real publications.

## Services offered

### Sector landscapes (typical project: $15K-$35K)
Complete cross-system analysis of a single sector or theme in a given
jurisdiction. Who&rsquo;s operating, how money flows, where the gaps are,
what the evidence says. Delivered as a written report plus underlying data.

### Custom entity resolution and analysis (typical project: $8K-$25K)
Targeted work for a specific question: market analysis for a procurement
team, cohort identification for a grant program, supply chain analysis for
a compliance function, or due diligence dossiers on specific organisations
at scale.

### Data licensing and API access (typical: $500-$3K/month)
Bulk data or live API access for research institutions, journalism
investigations, and government analytics teams. Scoped to the specific use
case with appropriate terms.

### Ongoing research partnership (typical: $50K-$120K/year)
Retained-style relationship for agencies or universities who want
continuous access to analytical capacity and the first look at new
investigations in their domain.

### Speaking, briefings, and workshops
One-off briefings for boards, executive teams, or research groups. Day
rate $2,500, travel at cost.

## Preferred engagement model

- Fixed-price projects for defined outputs. No open-ended hourly work.
- Scope defined up-front in a 1-page memo. Changes handled as variations.
- Deliverables in formats that survive the team change: Markdown + CSV +
  SQL queries commented for reuse. No black-box tooling.
- Indigenous advisory review for anything that touches First Nations
  data. Non-negotiable. Builds 2-4 weeks into the timeline.
