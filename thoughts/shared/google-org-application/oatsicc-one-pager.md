# AI-Powered Accountability for First Nations Children's Rights

## A Partnership Proposal: OATSICC + CivicGraph + Empathy Ledger

---

### The Opportunity

Google.org is investing $30M in AI solutions that transform public services. CivicGraph, an Australian social enterprise, is applying for $1-3M to build **AI-powered accountability infrastructure** for children's rights commissioners — and we want OATSICC as our government partner.

**Why OATSICC?** You are Australia's first dedicated Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Children's Commissioner with a statutory mandate to hold systems accountable. Your Strategic Plan identifies exactly the capabilities we've built: data-driven policy intelligence, evidence-based advocacy, and community voice in decision-making.

**What's in it for OATSICC?** Google-funded AI tools purpose-built for your mandate — at zero cost to your office. Plus Google Cloud credits, pro bono Google AI engineers, and global visibility for OATSICC's work.

---

### What We'd Build Together

**The Commissioner's AI Toolkit** — three integrated AI capabilities serving all five OATSICC strategic goals:

#### 1. Funding Intelligence (CivicGraph)

AI agents that continuously map every dollar flowing through Queensland's child protection, youth justice, disability, family services, and community sectors.

**What you'd see:**
- Which LGAs are funding deserts (we've already found 10 Very Remote QLD LGAs with maximum desert scores — Aurukun, Napranum, Torres, Carpentaria, Croydon)
- Where $1.88B in detention spending goes vs $1.49B in community supervision
- Which community-controlled organisations are monitored by multiple systems but receive minimal procurement funding (national average: 2.15 systems, 3.2% of procurement dollars)
- Cross-system power mapping: who contracts, donates, lobbies, and governs simultaneously

**Already built:** 21,739 QLD entities linked across 7 government systems. 65,607 justice funding records. Live funding desert scores for every Queensland LGA.

*Serves Goals 1, 3, 4*

#### 2. Community Voice Intelligence (Empathy Ledger)

A culturally safe platform where Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander children, families, and communities share their experiences — with AI that identifies systemic themes and cross-references them against funding data.

**What you'd see:**
- Community stories organized by theme, location, and system
- AI-detected patterns: "14 stories from Cairns region describe lack of cultural support in out-of-home care, but $0 flows to cultural connection programs in those LGAs"
- Children's voices directly informing policy briefs with cultural safety protocols

**Already built:** Multi-tenant storytelling platform with cultural sensitivity protocols, privacy controls, and 22,000+ knowledge base chunks processed.

*Serves Goals 2, 5*

#### 3. Evidence Intelligence (ALMA + CivicGraph)

AI that matches communities to evidence-based interventions: "For this community's profile, here are the programs with proven outcomes — and here's what they'd cost vs current detention spending."

**What you'd see:**
- 487 QLD-linked interventions with outcome evidence (37 diversion programs, 65 cultural connection, 65 community-led)
- Diversion pathway recommendations: "Aurukun has 0 funded diversion programs. Three evidence-based programs have demonstrated outcomes in similar Very Remote communities. Estimated cost: $X/year. Current detention cost per child: $Y/day."
- Policy brief auto-generation: AI drafts evidence-backed briefings for ministerial submissions

**Already built:** 1,162 interventions nationally in the Australian Living Map of Alternatives (ALMA), linked to CivicGraph entities with outcome evidence.

*Serves Goals 1, 3*

### Indigenous Data Sovereignty

We embed CARE principles (Collective benefit, Authority to control, Responsibility, Ethics) into the architecture from day 1:

- **Community consent layers** — First Nations communities control what data about them is accessible and to whom
- **Data governance board** with First Nations majority representation
- **Benefit-sharing** — insights serve communities first, not external parties
- **Cultural protocols** — AI outputs reviewed through cultural safety lens before publication
